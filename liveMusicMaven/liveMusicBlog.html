Live Music Maven

Daniela Lugo • April 18, 2023 • Adult Cohort

How One Direction Sparked My Passion for Live Music

As a member of the generation that grew up with the internet, I spent countless hours online watching everything from funny videos to beauty tutorials and music clips. However, it wasn't until I stumbled upon "One Direction - Video Diaries Funny Moments" that my passion for live music truly ignited.

In 2013, One Direction announced their Where We Are Tour for the following year, and as a devoted fan, I was determined to see them perform live. At just 13 years old, and unable to afford concert tickets, I knew I had to find a way to earn the money. I landed a job and set my sights on purchasing floor seats for the show. When ticket sales opened, I feverishly refreshed my computer screen and, miraculously, managed to snag a coveted spot in section B, row 10 for the August 17, 2014 concert. I felt incredibly fortunate to have secured my ticket, especially since they sold out in mere seconds.

As I eagerly awaited the concert, I used it as motivation to excel in school, telling myself, "If you do well, you can see One Direction." But regardless of my academic performance (it was good), I knew I was going to that concert.

That night was pure magic, and the experience sparked a deep love for live music within me. I found myself craving more and more concerts, and I began to prioritize attending as many shows as possible. Looking back, that One Direction concert was just the beginning of my live music journey - one that has become an essential part of my life.

The Magic of Music: How Attending Live Events Sparks Joy and Motivation

Since 2013, attending concerts and music festivals has become a powerful motivator for me, whether it's excelling in school, work, or any other aspect of life that requires discipline. While I'm content with attending at least one live event per year, I'm happiest when I can attend multiple.

Beyond just being a source of enjoyment, live music has also played a significant role in supporting my mental health and overall motivation. As the AARP notes, music can have a positive effect on brain chemicals like dopamine and oxytocin, which are associated with pleasure and positive emotions.

Knowing this, I see attending live music events as a form of self-care and validation for my desire to attend as many as possible. Whether it's a local concert or a large-scale music festival, the experience of hearing live music and feeling the energy of a crowd is truly transformative.

How to Prepare for a Live Music Event (Concert vs Music Festival)

While preparing for a concert and a multiple-day music festival share some similarities, there are also distinct differences. When preparing for a concert, your main concern may be choosing the perfect outfit for the day of the event. However, if you plan on bringing a bag, it's important to check the venue's bag policy beforehand, as they often have strict guidelines regarding bag sizes.

Whether you're preparing for a concert or a music festival, there are some key similarities to keep in mind. First and foremost, it's crucial to eat and stay hydrated before and during the event, as dehydration and hunger can cause fainting and other health issues. Additionally, if you plan on driving, it's wise to purchase a parking spot ahead of time, as this can be much cheaper than paying for parking on the day of the event. If you're using a rideshare service like Uber or Lyft, be aware that prices are likely to surge after the event due to high demand. In the case of a multi-day festival, consider purchasing a shuttle pass from the festival's website, as this can be a cost-effective alternative to relying on rideshares. Finally, regardless of the type of event, it's important to prioritize comfortable footwear to ensure you can enjoy the experience without any discomfort or pain.

The planning process for a concert versus a music festival can be vastly different. If you're attending a music festival, it's important to budget and save up beforehand. A general admission pass for a music festival can cost around $300 or more, not including taxes and additional fees. Other significant expenses to consider include accommodations, travel, food for the duration of the festival, festival attire and footwear, and essential items. It's also crucial to plan ahead and research the artists who will be performing, as there will likely be a wide variety of acts to choose from, and some of your favorites may be scheduled to perform at the same time.

Music Festival Essentials

As a seasoned festival goer, I've curated a short list of essential items to bring with you. First and foremost, it's crucial to stay hydrated, so I highly recommend investing in a hydration backpack. These backpacks feature a reusable, refillable pouch that can hold up to 2L of water, ensuring you stay hydrated throughout the day. Additionally, it's important to protect yourself from sun damage, especially during the spring and summer festival season. I recommend bringing SPF/sunblock to apply throughout the day. If you're camping on festival grounds, be sure to bring an appropriately sized tent and air mattress to ensure you get enough rest to enjoy the festival to the fullest.
